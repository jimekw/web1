CREATE A CUSTOM BAG

Assembly is required. But it’s simpler then it seems.

Figuring out what to get, how much and where the items go can be overwhelming. So rather then throwing one long list at you we organized all the items into eight main categories.

Water

Water is one of the most essential things one needs to survive and sustain life. A person needs a bare minimum of around a quarter of a gallon or one liter is ideal per day per person. But water can be heavy so it is more practical to find a way to purify water to drink. Water filtration or purification system one of the most important part of a BOB.

  • Water bottle
  • Iodine or purification tablets
  • A way to boil water
  • Water filter
  • Collapsible water bottle for extra storage
  • Hydration water bladder

    Shelter

    Shelter and a good sleeping bag are important for protection from the elements. A warm dry place to sleep can do a lot and in a high stress situationit is even more crucial to be rested.

    Here are some basic items for a first aid kit but remember that some people might have medical and conditions that will require other items that are not on this list such as perscription medications.

    Basic Gear

    This is a list of some basic gear you made need. Its a very braod range of items so breaking them up into sub groups is very helpful. Fist is light is important because in an emergency situation there may not be electricity or your not even near civilization at all. Next is something to make fire and I recommend flint and still because it can still work even if it gets wet. Lastly communication and positioning there are a number of things you can have ready depending on the type of emergency. It there are still cell towers working a cell phone is great and remember your charger. If not a good ol’ walkie talki and compass and map can help you communicate with your group and orient yourself to your surroundings.

    Important Papers

    Copies of important papers and documents is a valuable part of a BOB that many people forget. One solution is to have originals or copies in a safe at your local bank. But if the disaster has effected the entire community it may be better to keep copies on your person. Now with the digital age you can store valuable documents and backups on flashdrive and in the cloud.
